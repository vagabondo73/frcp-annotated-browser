Eleventh Amendment — Sovereign Immunity

Layer: CONSTITUTION · Category: Jurisdiction
The Judicial power of the United States shall not be construed to extend to any suit in law or equity, commenced or prosecuted against one of the United States by Citizens of another State, or by Citizens or Subjects of any Foreign State.
Notes: The Eleventh Amendment codifies state sovereign immunity in federal court, broadly interpreted to bar suits against unconsenting states by their own citizens as well (Hans v. Louisiana, 1890). Relevant to 12(b)(1) motions to dismiss and limits on federal court jurisdiction over state defendants.
