Full Faith and Credit Clause

Layer: CONSTITUTION · Category: Judgment
Full Faith and Credit shall be given in each State to the public Acts, Records, and judicial Proceedings of every other State. And the Congress may by general Laws prescribe the Manner in which such Acts, Records and Proceedings shall be proved, and the Effect thereof.
Notes: The Full Faith and Credit Clause requires states to recognize and enforce judgments of sister-state courts, enforced federally by 28 U.S.C. § 1738. It underlies issue and claim preclusion doctrine across state lines.
