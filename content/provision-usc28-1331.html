28 U.S.C. § 1331 — Federal Question Jurisdiction

Layer: STATUTE · Category: Jurisdiction
The district courts shall have original jurisdiction of all civil actions arising under the Constitution, laws, or treaties of the United States.
Notes: Enacted 1875, amended to remove amount-in-controversy requirement in 1980 (P.L. 96-486). The 'well-pleaded complaint rule' (Louisville & Nashville R.R. v. Mottley) limits § 1331 jurisdiction to cases where the federal question appears on the face of the plaintiff's properly pleaded complaint.

Landmark Cases (2)

Louisville & Nashville Railroad Co. v. Mottley, 211 U.S. 149 (1908)

Holding: Federal-question jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 1331 requires that the federal question appear on the face of a well-pleaded complaint; a federal defense is insufficient.

Established the well-pleaded complaint rule for federal-question jurisdiction.

Grable & Sons Metal Products, Inc. v. Darue Engineering & Manufacturing, 545 U.S. 308 (2005)

Holding: Federal-question jurisdiction over state-law claims exists when the state-law claim necessarily raises a disputed and substantial federal issue that a federal forum may entertain without disturbing the congressionally approved balance.

Refined the 'embedded federal question' doctrine beyond the well-pleaded complaint rule.
