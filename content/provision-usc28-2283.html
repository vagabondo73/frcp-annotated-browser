28 U.S.C. § 2283 — Anti-Injunction Act

Layer: STATUTE · Category: General
A court of the United States may not grant an injunction to stay proceedings in a State court except as expressly authorized by Act of Congress, or where necessary in aid of its jurisdiction, or to protect or effectuate its judgments.
Notes: The Anti-Injunction Act establishes a general prohibition on federal court injunctions against state court proceedings, with three narrow exceptions. Atlantic Coast Line R.R. v. Brotherhood of Locomotive Engineers (1970) established strict construction. The 'relitigation exception' (Chick Kam Choo v. Exxon Corp.) allows federal courts to protect their judgments from state court relitigation.
