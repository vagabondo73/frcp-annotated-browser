28 U.S.C. § 2284 — Three-Judge Court

Layer: STATUTE · Category: General
(a) A district court of three judges shall be convened when otherwise required by Act of Congress, or when an action is filed challenging the constitutionality of the apportionment of congressional districts or the apportionment of any statewide legislative body. (b) In any action required to be heard and determined by a district court of three judges under subsection (a) of this section, the composition and procedure of the court shall be as follows: (1) Upon the filing of a request for three judges, the judge to whom the request is presented shall, unless he determines that three judges are not required, immediately notify the chief judge of the circuit, who shall designate two other judges... (2) Of the three judges thus convened, each may administer oaths, examine witnesses, receive evidence, and act in all procedural matters... (3) The court shall hear and determine the action, and shall convene at the time and place fixed by the presiding judge. The clerk of the district court shall serve as the clerk of the three-judge court...
Notes: Three-judge district courts are required for congressional redistricting challenges. Appeals from three-judge courts go directly to the Supreme Court under 28 U.S.C. § 1253.
