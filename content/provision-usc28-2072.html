28 U.S.C. § 2072 — Rules Enabling Act

Layer: STATUTE · Category: Scope/Purpose
(a) The Supreme Court shall have the power to prescribe general rules of practice and procedure and rules of evidence for cases in the United States district courts (including proceedings before magistrate judges thereof) and courts of appeals. (b) Such rules shall not abridge, enlarge or modify any substantive right. All laws in conflict with such rules shall be of no further force or effect after such rules have taken effect. (c) Such rules may define when a ruling of a district court is final for the purposes of appeal under section 1291 of this title.
Notes: The Rules Enabling Act (originally enacted 1934) authorizes the Supreme Court to promulgate the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure. The constraint that rules 'shall not abridge, enlarge or modify any substantive right' is the basis for the Hanna v. Plumer (1965) test: if an FRCP directly covers the issue, the rule applies in federal court so long as it is within the Rules Enabling Act's authorization.

Landmark Cases (2)

Hanna v. Plumer, 380 U.S. 460 (1965)

Holding: When a Federal Rule of Civil Procedure directly governs the issue, it applies if it is valid under the Rules Enabling Act. The 'twin aims of Erie' — discouragement of forum shopping and avoidance of inequitable administration of the laws — guide only 'unguided Erie choices.'

Created the two-track Erie framework: REA track for FRCP rules, modified outcome-determination for judge-made rules.

Shady Grove Orthopedic Associates, P.A. v. Allstate Insurance Co., 559 U.S. 393 (2010)

Holding: Rule 23 governs whether a case may proceed as a class action in federal court, even if a state statute would prohibit class treatment. Rule 23 is valid under the Rules Enabling Act.

Major Erie/class-action intersection; confirmed FRCP 23 preempts contrary state class-action restrictions in federal court.
